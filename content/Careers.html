We need YOU!

Do you want to make an impact? Do you want to help people? Do you want to do something that touches the life of every human being?

If so, consider a career in food safety! The CDC estimates that every year, 1 in 6 Americans will suffer from a foodborne illness. In a food safety career, you would be able to work towards protecting the world from foodborne illnesses.

Since the Food Safety Modernization Act was signed into law in 2014, there is a great need for trained, competent employees in the food industry.

We need YOU to step up and protect our nation. With a degree in Food Science you can help keep your friends, family, and nation safe by preventing foodborne illness outbreaks.

Do you have what it takes?

Are you:

Passionate
Driven
Dedicated

The industry needs passionate, driven individuals that are dedicated to keeping a safe food source.

Do you have in interest in health, math, food, science, regulation, or serving the public? Theses are all areas that are integrated into a career in food safety. If this sounds like you, then earning a degree in Food Science is the next step to a career in Food Safety.

What will you gain from pursing a career in Food Safety?

  1. Foundational Knowledge
    Understand the microbiology, chemistry, and engineering principles behind food and food processing techniques.

  2. Career Mobility
    You can work virtually anywhere along the production chain- from working for an ingredient company or a meat processor to working for the government. There are so many different job titles to choose from!

  3. Job Security
    There will always be a need for qualified employees in the area of food safety because we will always need safe food.

  4. A Comfortable Living
    The reported median salary for food scientists is just under $60,000 per year.

What do you do now?

Enroll in a Food Science degree program!

There are many scholarship opportunities within the food science field for eager students.

You’ll be able to earn a degree in 4 years or less! And at NC State you can earn a Food Safety Manager Certificate while you complete your regular coursework.

Most importantly, you’ll graduate ready for a career almost anywhere around the country or locally. North Carolina is home to the headquarters for many companies including: Krispy Kreme, Mt. Olive Pickle Company, and Lowes Foods.
